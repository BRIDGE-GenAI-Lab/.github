# Security Policy

BRIDGE GenAI Lab takes security seriously — both in our research and in our code. We appreciate responsible disclosure of vulnerabilities.

## Reporting a Vulnerability

If you discover a security vulnerability in any of our repositories, **please do not open a public issue.** Instead:

1. **Email us** at mahmudomar70@gmail.com with the subject line: `[SECURITY] <brief description>`
2. Include:
   - Repository and branch affected
   - Steps to reproduce the vulnerability
   - Potential impact assessment
   - Suggested fix (if you have one)

We will acknowledge your report within **48 hours** and provide a detailed response within **7 business days**, including our assessment and planned remediation timeline.

## Scope

This policy applies to all repositories under the [BRIDGE-GenAI-Lab](https://github.com/BRIDGE-GenAI-Lab) organization.

### In Scope

- Code vulnerabilities in our tools, frameworks, and libraries
- Security issues in our evaluation harnesses or benchmarks
- Dependency vulnerabilities that affect our projects
- Data leakage or privacy concerns in our datasets

### Out of Scope

- Vulnerabilities in third-party dependencies (report these to the upstream maintainer)
- Social engineering attacks
- Denial of service attacks against our infrastructure

## Responsible Disclosure Guidelines

- Give us reasonable time to address the issue before public disclosure
- Make a good faith effort to avoid privacy violations, data destruction, or disruption of services
- Do not access or modify data that does not belong to you

## A Note on Our Research

Our lab publishes research on AI agent security, including tool poisoning and adversarial attacks. The attack techniques described in our papers are disclosed responsibly and are intended to improve defenses. If you have concerns about the security implications of our published research, we welcome that conversation — reach out at the email above.

## Recognition

We gratefully acknowledge security researchers who responsibly disclose vulnerabilities. With your permission, we will credit you in our release notes and in this document.
