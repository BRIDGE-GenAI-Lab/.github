# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

BRIDGE GenAI Lab is committed to fostering an open, welcoming, and inclusive research environment. We are dedicated to making participation in our projects a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

**Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:**

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Respecting differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community and for scientific rigor
- Showing empathy toward other community members
- Providing proper attribution for ideas, code, and research contributions

**Examples of unacceptable behavior:**

- The use of sexualized language or imagery and unwelcome sexual attention
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Misrepresenting research findings or fabricating data
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional research setting

## Responsible Research

As a lab focused on AI security, we hold ourselves to an additional standard:

- We conduct adversarial research responsibly, with the goal of improving defenses
- We follow responsible disclosure practices for all vulnerabilities discovered
- We consider the broader implications of our published attacks and mitigations
- We do not release tools or techniques solely designed to cause harm without corresponding defenses

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces — including GitHub repositories, issues, pull requests, discussions, and any public forums where an individual is representing the lab or its projects.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by contacting the lab lead at mahmudomar70@gmail.com. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. The project team is obligated to maintain confidentiality with regard to the reporter of an incident.
